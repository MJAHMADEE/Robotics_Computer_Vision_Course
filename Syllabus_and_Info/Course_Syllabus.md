## Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive exploration of the intersection between robotics, computer vision, and modern artificial intelligence. Students will master spatial transformations and kinematics fundamentals, implement classical and modern computer vision pipelines, deploy deep learning models (CNNs, Transformers, ViTs) for visual perception, and integrate these technologies into embodied robotic systems.

The course emphasizes **practical, systems-level integration** leveraging Python (OpenCV, PyTorch, TensorFlow) and MATLAB. Students will transition from theoretical foundations to real-world applications, culminating in capstone projects that merge kinematics, vision, and deep learning.

---

## Course Objectives

By completing this course, students will:

1. ✅ **Master spatial representations** used in robotics (rotation matrices, quaternions, homogeneous transforms)
2. ✅ **Analyze kinematic chains** and solve forward/inverse kinematic problems
3. ✅ **Implement classical vision algorithms** (filtering, morphology, calibration)
4. ✅ **Design and train neural networks** from MLPs to modern Vision Transformers
5. ✅ **Deploy real-time object detection** systems (YOLO)
6. ✅ **Implement tracking algorithms** (optical flow, Kalman filtering)
7. ✅ **Integrate vision-language models** into robotic decision-making
8. ✅ **Execute end-to-end systems engineering projects**

## Course Structure & Topics

### Phase 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-5)
- **Week 1-2**: Spatial Description & Transformations
- **Week 2-3**: Homogeneous Transformations (SE(3))
- **Week 3-5**: Kinematics Fundamentals (FK, IK, Dynamics)

### Phase 2: Classical Vision (Weeks 5-7)
- **Week 5-6**: Classical Computer Vision (Calibration, Filtering)
- **Week 6-7**: Image Processing, Morphology, Color Spaces

### Phase 3: Deep Learning & Vision (Weeks 7-11)
- **Week 7**: Neural Network Foundations
- **Week 8**: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
- **Week 8-9**: Object Detection (YOLO, SSD, R-CNN)
- **Week 9-10**: Object Tracking & Temporal Analysis
- **Week 10**: Transformers in Vision (ViT, DeiT)
- **Week 11**: Advanced Segmentation (Mask R-CNN, SAM)

### Phase 4: Applications (Weeks 12)
- **Week 12**: Modern Embodied AI & Systems Integration

## Course Materials

### Required Resources
- Python 3.8+ with Jupyter Notebook
- OpenCV 4.5+ for computer vision
- PyTorch 2.0+ for deep learning
- NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib for scientific computing
- MATLAB (optional, for control design)

### Recommended Textbooks

1. **Robotics & Kinematics**
   - Craig, J.J. (2005). *Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control*
   - Corke, P.I. (2011). *Robotics, Vision and Control*
   - Spong, M.W., et al. (2006). *Robot Modeling and Control*

2. **Computer Vision**
   - Forsyth, D. & Ponce, J. (2012). *Computer Vision: A Modern Approach*
   - Hartley, R. & Zisserman, A. (2003). *Multiple View Geometry in CV*

3. **Deep Learning & Machine Learning**
   - Goodfellow, I., Bengio, Y., & Courville, A. (2016). *Deep Learning*
   - Géron, A. (2019). *Hands-On Machine Learning* (2nd ed.)
