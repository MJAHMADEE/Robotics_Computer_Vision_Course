# 📌 Course Policies & Expectations

## Academic Integrity

### Core Principles
All students are expected to uphold the highest standards of academic integrity, consistent with KNTU's institutional policies and the Iranian Code of Academic Ethics.

### What Constitutes Academic Dishonesty
- **Plagiarism**: Representing another's work, ideas, or code as your own without attribution
- **Cheating**: Using unauthorized materials, communication, or assistance during exams
- **Unauthorized Collaboration**: Submitting work that violates collaboration guidelines
- **Fabrication**: Creating or falsifying data, citations, or results
- **Misrepresentation**: Misleading instructors about the nature of your work

### Approved Collaboration Guidelines

#### ✅ **Allowed Collaboration**
- Discussing general problem concepts and approaches with classmates
- Explaining your debugging process and helping others debug logic (not code)
- Working on mini-projects as official team assignments
- Consulting textbooks, online tutorials, and course materials
- Using Stack Overflow, GitHub, and documentation for learning concepts

#### ❌ **Prohibited Collaboration**
- Sharing code, solutions, or assignment submissions
- Copying code from other students or the internet and submitting as original
- Having someone else write or debug your code
- Submitting work meant for another course
- Failing to cite external code sources or algorithms

### Smart Guides vs. Solutions
- **Smart Guides**: Encouraged to use! Hints and logical direction without spoilers
- **Solutions**: For learning after you've submitted; reference only
- **Copying Solutions**: Prohibited and will result in grade penalties

### Consequences of Academic Dishonesty
| Severity | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|----------|---|---|---|
| Minor Plagiarism | -50% assignment grade | -100% assignment, F in course | Referral to Dean |
| Code Copying | Failed assignment + written warning | -1 full letter grade in course | Academic probation |
| Exam Cheating | F on exam | F in course | Dismissal proceedings |
| Systematic Plagiarism | F in course | Academic probation | Dismissal |

## Attendance & Participation

### Lecture Attendance
- Lectures are **mandatory** and attendance will be tracked
- **Excused Absences**: Illness, family emergency, or religious observance (documentation required)
- **Chronic Absenteeism**: After 3 unexcused absences, may be reported; after 6, course withdrawal initiated

### Lab/Hands-on Sessions
- Lab time is critical for hands-on learning and troubleshooting
- Lab attendance requirement: Minimum 80% attendance
- Make-up labs available for excused absences only

### Participation Grade
- Active engagement in class discussions and Q&A
- Thoughtful questions and peer help on course forum
- Constructive tone and respectful communication
- **Up to +2 bonus points** available for exceptional participation

## Assignment Submission & Deadlines

### Submission Format
Assignments must be submitted as:
1. **Code**: Well-commented Python scripts or Jupyter notebooks
2. **Report**: PDF or Markdown document with:
   - Problem statement understanding
   - Approach and methodology
   - Results and visualizations
   - Conclusions and insights
   - References to consulted resources

### Deadline Policy
- **On-Time** (by 11:59 PM due date): Full credit
- **1-2 days late**: -20% deduction
- **3-5 days late**: -50% deduction
- **>5 days late**: No credit (0 points)

**Exception**: Documented medical or family emergency → 1 extension of 3 days

### Resubmission Policy
- Assignments cannot be resubmitted after grading
- If you receive feedback, incorporate it in subsequent assignments
- Exam corrections not permitted

## Exam Policies

### Midterm Exam
- **Date**: Mid-March 2026 (announced 2 weeks in advance)
- **Format**: Written + Computational components
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours
- **Materials**: Approved formula sheets (1 page, handwritten only)
- **Calculator**: Basic calculator allowed; no graphing or programmable

### Exam Conduct
- **Cheating**: Zero tolerance. Any form of cheating results in failure
- **Accommodations**: Students with disabilities should notify instructor in first week
- **Makeup Exams**: Available only for documented emergencies; arranged within 1 week

### Exam Grading
- Partial credit awarded for methodology, even if final answer incorrect
- Common mistakes discussed in class after exam
- Grade review available by appointment within 1 week of return

## Final Project Guidelines

### Project Scope
- **Individual or Small Teams** (2-3 students)
- **Duration**: 4 weeks of focused development
- **Deliverables**: Code + Report + Presentation

### Evaluation Criteria
| Criteria | Points | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Technical Correctness** | 3 pts | Algorithm implementation, functionality |
| **Code Quality** | 2 pts | Style, documentation, edge cases |
| **Experimental Results** | 2 pts | Validation, benchmarking, comparisons |
| **Report & Presentation** | 1 pt | Clarity, organization, professionalism |

### Project Selection
Students must select one of 3 tracks:
1. **Surgical Instrument Tracking** – Vision-based tracking in surgical environments
2. **Autonomous Navigation** – Visual SLAM and obstacle avoidance
3. **Robotic Manipulation** – Vision-guided object grasping and placement
